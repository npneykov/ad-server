Why Banner Ad Sizes Matter

Choosing the right banner ad sizes can make or break your advertising campaign. Standard IAB (Interactive Advertising Bureau) sizes ensure your ads display correctly across ad networks, achieve better fill rates, and reach the widest possible audience. Non-standard sizes often result in lower CPMs, poor ad delivery, and wasted creative resources.

In this guide, we'll cover the most effective banner ad sizes for 2026, their performance benchmarks, and when to use each format.

The 6 Most Important Banner Ad Sizes

1. 728x90 Leaderboard

The leaderboard is the most popular desktop banner format. It typically appears at the top of web pages, above the fold, making it highly visible.

  • Best for: Desktop traffic, header placements, blog posts
  • Average CTR: 0.3-0.5%
  • Fill rate: 95%+ (extremely high demand from advertisers)
  • When to use: Primary desktop placement, especially above content

2. 300x250 Medium Rectangle

The medium rectangle is the most versatile banner size, performing well on both desktop and mobile. It can be embedded within content or placed in sidebars.

  • Best for: In-content ads, sidebar placements, mobile responsive layouts
  • Average CTR: 0.4-0.6%
  • Fill rate: 95%+ (highest fill rate of all sizes)
  • When to use: Any placement, especially when mobile traffic is significant

3. 320x50 Mobile Banner

The mobile banner is optimized for smartphone screens. It's the go-to size for mobile-first websites and apps.

  • Best for: Mobile-only traffic, footer placements, sticky banners
  • Average CTR: 0.5-0.8% (higher than desktop due to accidental clicks)
  • Fill rate: 90%+
  • When to use: Any mobile placement, especially sticky headers/footers

4. 160x600 Wide Skyscraper

The wide skyscraper is a vertical banner designed for desktop sidebar placements. Its tall format makes it highly visible during scrolling.

  • Best for: Desktop sidebars, content-heavy sites with long articles
  • Average CTR: 0.2-0.4%
  • Fill rate: 80%+
  • When to use: Right-hand sidebar on desktop layouts

5. 468x60 Full Banner

One of the oldest banner formats, still widely used for above-fold and below-content placements on desktop.

  • Best for: Desktop traffic, footer areas, email newsletters
  • Average CTR: 0.3-0.5%
  • Fill rate: 85%+
  • When to use: Secondary desktop placements, legacy sites

6. 160x300 Half-Page

A compact vertical banner that fits well in narrow sidebars without being as tall as the 160x600.

  • Best for: Desktop sidebars, narrow layouts
  • Average CTR: 0.2-0.4%
  • Fill rate: 75%+
  • When to use: When sidebar space is limited

How to Choose the Right Banner Sizes

For Desktop-Only Sites

If your traffic is primarily desktop (70%+ desktop users), focus on these sizes:

  1. 728x90 leaderboard - Above the fold
  2. 300x250 medium rectangle - In-content or sidebar
  3. 160x600 wide skyscraper - Right sidebar (optional)

For Mobile-First Sites

If your traffic is primarily mobile (70%+ mobile users), prioritize:

  1. 320x50 mobile banner - Sticky header or footer
  2. 300x250 medium rectangle - In-content placements

For Balanced Desktop + Mobile Traffic

For sites with mixed traffic, use responsive ad containers with these fallbacks:

  1. 728x90 on desktop, 320x50 on mobile (header)
  2. 300x250 (universal, works everywhere)
  3. 468x60 on desktop, 320x50 on mobile (footer)

Common Banner Size Mistakes to Avoid

1. Using Non-Standard Sizes

Custom sizes like 500x200 or 250x250 may work for direct campaigns, but they'll have terrible fill rates on ad networks. Stick to IAB standard sizes.

2. Only Using One Size

Relying on a single banner size limits your ad inventory. Use at least 2-3 sizes per page to maximize fill rates and revenue.

3. Ignoring Mobile Sizes

With 60%+ of web traffic coming from mobile in 2026, failing to implement 320x50 mobile banners means losing significant revenue.

4. Poor Placement Choices

Even the best banner size won't perform if placed poorly. Place 728x90 leaderboards above the fold, 300x250 ads within content, and avoid placing ads in areas users rarely see.

Conclusion

Understanding standard banner ad sizes is essential for any publisher or advertiser. The 300x250 medium rectangle and 728x90 leaderboard remain the top performers in 2026, but diversifying your ad inventory with mobile-specific sizes like 320x50 can significantly boost revenue.
